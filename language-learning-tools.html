What do I use to learn languages?


I've loved learning languages since I was a little kid. I've developed some routines and found some tools that help me stay on top of things and progress. Here are some of those things for some of the languages I've been learning. A number of those tools are good to use when you've already reached a level of fluency (A2/B1 is enough, typically). I marked them with an asterisk. I've written this in English for simplicity, and some of the materials are also in English. They're segregated by language. I hope you find them useful - I know I have.

German

  • Nachrichtenleicht* - news in simple German, from German Public Broadcaster. Each news item has a wordlist and an audio recording, and there's a separate wordbook;
  • Wiktionary - an online dictionary. Search the word and choose the language. Offers sample sentences and contexts.
  • Becoming Fluent in German: 150 Short Stories*, Philipp Eich - short stories, each with a dedicated wordlist;

Swedish

  • Radio Sweden på lätt svenska* - news in easy Swedish, from the Swedish Public Broadcaster. Each news item also has an audio recording. Articles don't have their own wordlists, but the next item on my list will help with that;
  • Wiktionary - an online dictionary. Search the word and choose the language. Offers conjugation, sample sentences and contexts;
  • Svenska Akademiens Ordböcker* - an official dictionary of the Swedish Academy. Provides word definitions (in Swedish), as well as conjugation.

Arabic

  • Easy Arabic News* - news in easy Arabic, written by a translator from Syria. Not updated all that often, but I find it useful to practice language in context. Language in different levels;
  • Wiktionary - an online dictionary. Search the word and choose the language. Offers conjugation, sample sentences and contexts
  • Arabic Stories for Language Leaners, Hezi Brosh, Lutfi Mansur - bilingual (English-Arabic) short stories of Arab and Islamic heritage. Light, easy language - a good introduction to Arabic in context;
  • 501 Arabic verbs, Barron's Foreign Language Guides - incredibly useful when you're learning verbs. The guide offers full conjugation (including masdar), as well as sample sentences with the verb. I use it constantly to learn new words, as well as doublecheck when I'm writing.
