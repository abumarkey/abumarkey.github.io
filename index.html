Hi! My name’s Marek Fronc.





I’m a mobile photographer, and blogger based in Kraków, Poland.

I’m interested in society, politics and pop-culture of the Middle East and North Africa. Before studying the region at a university (undergrad, class of 2022), I’d spent four years in Bahrain, where I graduated high school. I still occasionally visit.

As a photographer, I focus on city landscape, with a little twist of nature shots. I’ve been using smartphones to take photos since 2013 — with iPhones definitely as favourites.

To stay true to my maniacal minimalism, I also became an avid cyclist, having mostly ditched mass transit for a trekking bike (except when the occasional flat tire strikes).

I sometimes write about politics and society here. I also have a blog about the Middle East, which I update in Polish (on hiatus for now). I also speak Czech, and English, as well as learn Arabic and German.

An anarcho-socialist, and a feminist, I also support equal rights for LGBT+ and all ethnicities. Capitalism exploits us, and the ruling class seeks to divide us to divert our attention from it.
