Hi! My name’s Marek Fronc.





I am a mobile photographer based in Kraków, Poland.

My interests are histories, societies and politics in Middle East and North Africa. I focus on Persian Gulf, where I spent four years of my life. I still visit Bahrain, where I went to high school. Intrigued after returning to Poland, I finally began Middle East Studies, which I will graduate in 2022. For my thesis, I research nationalisms in Persian Gulf.

I used to write things online. I'm now coming back to it. In photography, I focus on city landscapes, with a little twist of nature shots. I have been using several smartphones to take photos since 2013. Instagram is where majority of my creative output now goes.

I like to think I have a knack for languages — I speak Polish, Czech and English fluently, as well as learn Arabic (إنْ شاء اللهُ) and German.

I'm also a minimalist and a militant cyclist, having mostly ditched mass transit for a trekking bike. We're cooler than the electric car drivers — especially in winter.

My other pursuits lie squarely in Humanities: contemporary politics, religions, as well as histories of Europe. An anarchist, and a feminist, I also support equal rights for LGBT+ and all ethnicities. Capitalism exploits us, and the ruling class seeks to divide us to divert our attention from it.
