Hi! My name’s Marek Fronc.





I am a mobile photographer based in southern Poland.

I am also a Middle East Studies graduate. I'm interested in sociopolitical relations and modern history of Middle East and North Africa — Persian Gulf in particular. My bachelor's thesis, written in 2022, explores militarisation of nationalisms in Saudi Arabia and UAE.

My interests comes from experiencing the Gulf in person. I had spent four years in Bahrain, where I attended high school. Since that time, I've also visited a number of other countries in the region — Qatar, UAE, and Oman (so far).

I've grown fascinated with learning languages. Apart from native Polish, and Czech I learnt in childhood, I became fluent in English. During my studies, I began studying Arabic, a journey I continue. In 2020, I also picked up German.

I am an amateur photographer, and smartphones are my tools. I'd used instagram since 2013. Because of Meta's harmful effect of on societies and my own mind, I now use behance instead. My other hobby is blogging — I launched a blog about Middle East in early 2022, currently in Polish.

Other labels that apply: a minimalist, a cyclist, and an anarchist who supports EU. Glory to Ukraine!
